# Mathematical and Formal Models of Consciousness and Emotion: A Research Survey

## TL;DR
- The field splits into two unevenly developed wings: **consciousness** has several explicit mathematical theories (IIT's Φ, Friston's free-energy/active inference, Hoffman's conscious-agent Markov kernels, Tegmark's perceptronium, category-theoretic qualia), but all remain contested or untested; **emotion** has more empirically grounded but less ambitious formalisms (Russell's circumplex geometry, the Ornstein–Uhlenbeck/DynAffect stochastic model, catastrophe theory, control-theory of affect, appraisal-based computational models).
- The single most rigorous and most adversarially tested theory is **Integrated Information Theory (IIT 4.0)**, whose Φ ("phi") measure is mathematically explicit — but a landmark 2025 *Nature* adversarial collaboration challenged both IIT and Global Workspace, and no theory yet survives as established fact.
- For DIRECTION 2, the most promising places to focus are: (1) the **Free Energy Principle / active inference** as a unifying bridge between consciousness AND emotion (it formalizes both via the same variational math); (2) **affect dynamics** (Ornstein–Uhlenbeck/DynAffect) as the most mature *equation for emotion*; and (3) **mathematical consciousness science** (Kleiner, Tsuchiya, Hoffman) as the live frontier where an "overlooked breakthrough" is most plausible.

## Key Findings

**Consciousness — explicit mathematical theories exist but none is established.** Integrated Information Theory is the most formally developed: it proposes a scalar Φ that quantifies irreducible cause–effect power. The Free Energy Principle reframes brains (and arguably consciousness) as variational-free-energy minimizers. Donald Hoffman's conscious-agent theory is fully formal (Markov kernels, polytopes) but radically idealist. Several physics-flavored proposals (Tegmark's perceptronium, McFadden's CEMI electromagnetic field, Penrose–Hameroff Orch-OR, Stapp and Fisher's quantum theories) exist on a spectrum from speculative to fringe.

**Emotion — formalisms are more empirically grounded but less philosophically ambitious.** Most emotion models do not attempt to explain subjective experience; they model the *structure* (circumplex/PAD geometry), *dynamics* (dynamical systems, stochastic differential equations, catastrophe theory), or *generation* (appraisal-based computational models) of affective states.

**The two wings are converging through predictive processing.** Barrett's theory of constructed emotion, Seth's interoceptive inference, and Friston's active inference all use the same Bayesian/variational machinery to handle perception, emotion, and selfhood — making this the strongest cross-cutting framework, and the natural mathematical bridge between the user's Direction 1 (needs/motivation) and Direction 2 (consciousness/emotion).

## Details

### A. CONSCIOUSNESS — mathematical/formal theories

**1. Integrated Information Theory (IIT) — Giulio Tononi, Christof Koch, Larissa Albantakis, Masafumi Oizumi, et al.**
IIT is the flagship mathematical theory of consciousness. It starts from phenomenological "axioms" (existence, intrinsicality, information, integration, exclusion, composition) translated into physical "postulates" applied to a substrate characterized by the transition probability function of its units. Its central quantity is **Φ ("big phi")**, which measures the irreducible integrated information of a system's maximally irreducible cause–effect structure. The latest version, **IIT 4.0** (Albantakis, Barbosa, Findlay, Grasso, Haun, Marshall, Mayner, Tononi et al., *PLOS Computational Biology* 2023; arXiv:2212.14787), introduces an "intrinsic difference" (ID) measure of intrinsic information and computes Φ as the sum of distinction and relation phi values (φd + φr) — the worked example in the IIT Wiki yields Φ = 3.21 + 0.93 = 4.14 for a toy system. Software (PyPhi) computes Φ for small systems. **Claimed breakthrough:** a single number that determines whether, how much, and in what way a system is conscious — implying panpsychist consequences (any system with non-zero Φ has some experience). **Status: contested but central.** It is mathematically rigorous yet practically incomputable for real brains (combinatorial explosion), and faces the "unfolding argument" (Doerig et al.) that it may be unfalsifiable. In June 2025 it was directly tested against Global Workspace.

**2. Global Workspace Theory / Global Neuronal Workspace (GNW) — Bernard Baars, Stanislas Dehaene, Jean-Pierre Changeux.**
GWT proposes that consciousness arises when information is "broadcast" to a global workspace. The **Dehaene–Changeux model (DCM)** is its computational implementation: a neural network of long-range cortico-cortical pyramidal neurons in which conscious access corresponds to a sudden, nonlinear, all-or-none **"ignition"** — modeled as a stochastic phase transition. Wallace and Werner have described ignition using statistical-physics tools (giant-component formation in dynamic networks, scaling and universality of non-equilibrium phase transitions). A 2020 bioRxiv preprint formalizes a "Predictive Global Neuronal Workspace" using active inference. **Status: mainstream and empirically productive, but the formalism is more simulation than closed-form theory.**

**3. The 2025 adversarial test (Cogitate Consortium, *Nature*).**
The Cogitate Consortium (Ferrante, Gorska-Klimowska, Henin et al., *Nature* vol. 642, 5 June 2025, pp. 133–142; DOI 10.1038/s41586-025-08888-1) ran a preregistered adversarial collaboration to directly test IIT vs. GNWT. Per the paper's own wording: "Human participants (n = 256) viewed suprathreshold stimuli for variable durations while neural activity was measured with functional magnetic resonance imaging, magnetoencephalography and intracranial electroencephalography." The work was run across six laboratories. **Result: both theories were challenged.** IIT's prediction of sustained posterior synchronization was not supported; GNWT was challenged by a general lack of prefrontal "ignition" at stimulus offset and limited prefrontal representation of conscious content. The data did corroborate IIT's broader claim that posterior cortex is sufficient for consciousness and that prefrontal involvement/global broadcasting are not necessary. This is the most important recent empirical development, and an ongoing iEEG/single-unit (monkey) replication is underway.

**4. Free Energy Principle & Active Inference — Karl Friston.**
The FEP holds that any self-organizing system that persists must minimize **variational free energy** F — an information-theoretic upper bound on "surprise" (negative log model evidence). The canonical form expresses F as expected energy minus entropy, or equivalently as **complexity minus accuracy**, and is minimized either by updating internal states (perception) or by acting (active inference). It builds on Helmholtz's "unconscious inference" and predictive coding. Recent "Bayesian mechanics" writes down equations of motion for any "thing" that minimizes free energy. Wanja Wiese, Friston, and others argue the FEP can supply the "X" that distinguishes genuinely conscious systems (e.g., requiring a sufficiently deep generative model enabling counterfactual/temporal-depth processing). **Status: highly influential, mathematically rich, but criticized for unfalsifiability and for conflating variational with thermodynamic free energy (Gottwald & Braun; the "two kinds of free energy" critique).**

**5. Conscious Agent Theory / Interface Theory of Perception — Donald Hoffman & Chetan Prakash.**
Hoffman defines a **conscious agent as a six-tuple** (X, G, W, P, D, A, plus a counter N) where perception P, decision D, and action A are **Markovian kernels** (probabilistic channels). Agents combine into larger agents and fuse into simpler ones; the possible dynamics of n agents form an n(n−1)-dimensional **Markov polytope Mₙ** with nⁿ vertices, and qualia fusions form an (n−1)-dimensional simplex. In "Fusions of Consciousness" (Hoffman, Prakash, Fields) and "Objects of Consciousness," he maps agent dynamics onto scattering amplitudes (decorated permutations, geometric algebra) to attempt to *derive spacetime* from networks of conscious agents. **Claimed breakthrough:** a rigorous solution to the combination problem and a derivation of physics from consciousness. **Status: fully formal but fringe/idealist;** the spacetime-derivation program is admittedly incomplete (Hoffman concedes it "might be precisely wrong"), and the framework inverts mainstream physicalism.

**6. Consciousness as a state of matter ("perceptronium") — Max Tegmark.**
Tegmark (*Chaos, Solitons & Fractals* 76:238–270, 2015; arXiv:1401.1219) generalizes IIT to arbitrary quantum systems, proposing that consciousness is a state of matter ("perceptronium") distinguished by several principles — **information, integration, independence, dynamics**, and (in a later version) **utility**. He links it to error-correcting codes, condensed-matter criticality, and the emergence of time, and raises the "integration paradox": a Hopfield net with ~10¹¹ neurons stores only ~37 bits, so in his words, "why does the information content of our conscious experience appear to be vastly larger than 37 bits?... This strongly implies that the integration principle must be supplemented by at least one additional principle." **Status: speculative thought-experiment; influential as a framing but not a working model.**

**7. Electromagnetic field theories — Johnjoe McFadden (CEMI), Susan Pockett, E. Roy John.**
McFadden's **Conscious Electromagnetic Information (CEMI) field theory** (*Journal of Consciousness Studies* 2002; updated *Neuroscience of Consciousness* 2020) claims the brain's EM field is the physical substrate of consciousness — digital neuronal information is "pooled and integrated" into the field, which then influences neuronal firing via voltage-gated ion channels (closing a causal loop). It offers a proposed solution to the binding problem (the field unifies disparate neuronal information). Susan Pockett and E. Roy John advanced related EM-field theories around the same time. **Status: a minority physicalist theory, more conceptual than mathematical, but with testable predictions (synchronous-firing correlates, exogenous-field effects).**

**8. Penrose–Hameroff Orchestrated Objective Reduction (Orch-OR).**
Roger Penrose and Stuart Hameroff propose that consciousness arises from quantum computations in neuronal **microtubules**, terminated by Penrose's gravitationally-induced **objective reduction (OR)** — quantum state collapse tied to spacetime geometry, occurring ~40 times/second in concert with gamma EEG. Tubulin proteins act as qubits evolving by the Schrödinger equation until the OR threshold (e.g., ~25 ms in gamma synchrony). The 2014 review ("Consciousness in the universe," *Physics of Life Reviews* 11(1):39–78) responds to criticisms. **Status: prominent but widely rejected.** Tegmark (*Phys. Rev. E* 61:4194, 2000) calculated microtubule decoherence times of ~10⁻¹³ s, far too short to be neurophysiologically relevant; Hagan, Hameroff & Tuszyński (*Phys. Rev. E* 65:061901, 2002; arXiv:quant-ph/0005025) rebut that Tegmark's calculation targets a "hybrid" model rather than the actual Orch-OR proposal and recompute decoherence times of 10⁻⁵–10⁻⁴ s (potentially extendable to 10⁻²–10⁻¹ s). The dispute remains unresolved.

**9. Quantum theories beyond Orch-OR — Henry Stapp; Matthew Fisher.**
Henry Stapp builds on the **von Neumann–Wigner interpretation**, proposing that conscious attention acting as rapid repeated measurement holds neural quantum states in place via the **Quantum Zeno Effect**, allowing intention to influence the brain (Schwartz, Stapp & Beauregard, *Phil. Trans. R. Soc. B* 360:1309–1327, 2005; Stapp, *Mindful Universe*, Springer 2007). **Status: fringe;** Danko Georgiev and others showed the QZE breaks down beyond the brain's decoherence time and that local projection operators cannot decrease the brain's von Neumann entropy, contradicting Stapp's decoherence-robustness claim. Matthew Fisher (*Annals of Physics* 362:593–602, 2015; arXiv:1508.05929) proposes **nuclear-spin quantum cognition**: phosphorus-31 nuclear spins as "neural qubits," entangled via pyrophosphate hydrolysis and protected from decoherence inside **Posner molecules** Ca₉(PO₄)₆, with quantum correlations modulating neurotransmitter release via Ca²⁺. **Status: speculative but testable — the most scientifically respectable quantum-mind proposal,** with follow-up quantum-information formalization by Yunger Halpern & Crosson (*Annals of Physics*, 2019; arXiv:1711.04801).

**10. Attention Schema Theory — Michael Graziano.**
AST proposes the brain builds a simplified **model ("schema") of its own attention**, analogous to the body schema; the content of this internal model leads the brain to claim it has subjective awareness. It is grounded in control theory (the schema improves top-down attention control). The control-theory prediction has been tested computationally: Wilterson & Graziano (*PNAS* 118(33):e2102421118, 2021) showed that a deep Q-learning agent "can be trained to control visual attention when it is given an attention schema, but its performance is greatly reduced when the schema is not available" — with an attention schema the agent learned to perform; without one "the machine was comparatively incapacitated." **Status: mechanistic, testable, materialist; a "deflationary"/illusionist theory** that explains the *claim* of consciousness rather than phenomenal experience itself.

**11. Predictive processing & interoceptive inference — Andy Clark, Jakob Hohwy, Anil Seth.**
Anil Seth's **"beast machine" / "controlled hallucination"** theory applies hierarchical predictive coding and active inference to *interoception*: emotions are the brain's best Bayesian guess about the causes of interoceptive (bodily) signals, and conscious selfhood is grounded in control-oriented interoceptive inference (allostasis). Key papers: Seth, "Interoceptive inference, emotion, and the embodied self," *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* 17(11):565–573 (2013); Seth & Friston, "Active interoceptive inference and the emotional brain," *Phil. Trans. R. Soc. B* 371:20160007 (2016); and *Being You* (2021). The formal content is variational free-energy minimization with autonomic reflexes "enslaved" by descending interoceptive predictions. **Status: influential and increasingly mainstream framework, under active empirical test.** This is the strongest bridge between the consciousness and emotion literatures.

**12. Mathematical/structural consciousness science — Johannes Kleiner, Naotsugu Tsuchiya, Hayato Saigo, Robert Prentner.**
A live frontier ("the structural turn") aims to define rigorously what a "mathematical structure of conscious experience" is. Kleiner ("Mathematical Models of Consciousness," *Entropy* 22(6):609, 2020; PhD thesis "Topics in Mathematical Consciousness Science," LMU Munich 2024) provides axiomatic, often category-theoretic definitions of IIT and active inference, derives the active-inference free-energy formula rigorously, and (with Tull) proposes amendments to IIT's formalism to overcome the Barrett & Mediano (2019) criticism. Tsuchiya, Saigo & colleagues use **category theory and the Yoneda lemma** to characterize qualia structure relationally (enriched categories modeling similarity judgments; *Neuroscience of Consciousness* 2021; *Neuroscience Research* 2016). Earlier roots: R.P. Stanley's "Qualia space" (*J. Consciousness Studies* 1999), W.C. Hoffman's Lie-algebra/subjective-geometry of perception (1966, 1980). **Status: nascent, mathematically serious, and the most plausible home for an "overlooked breakthrough."**

### B. EMOTION — mathematical/formal/computational models

**1. Dimensional/geometric models — Russell's circumplex; Mehrabian's PAD.**
James Russell's **circumplex model of affect** (*JPSP* 39(6):1161–1178, 1980) maps all affective states onto a 2-D circular space defined by **valence** (horizontal) and **arousal** (vertical), with distance from origin = intensity. It was derived by factor analysis and multidimensional scaling of emotion-word similarity ratings; emotions order around the circle (happy → excited → alarmed → tense → sad → bored → calm → content). Mehrabian's **PAD model** adds a third dimension, **dominance** (Pleasure–Arousal–Dominance). Russell & Barrett's "core affect" concept treats valence/arousal as the always-present primitive. **Status: mainstream and empirically robust; the dominant geometric formalism, widely used in affective computing.** A recent extension models transitions between circumplex regions as a **Markov chain** ("Affects affect affects," 2023).

**2. Affect dynamics / DynAffect — Peter Kuppens, Zita Oravecz, Francis Tuerlinckx.**
The most mature *equation for emotion*. The **DynAffect model** treats core affect as a bivariate **Ornstein–Uhlenbeck (OU) stochastic differential equation** (a mean-reverting "damped" process):
**dX(t) = β(μ − X(t)) dt + σ dW(t)**, where **μ** is the "home base"/attractor, **β** is attractor strength (regulation speed; low β = high "emotional inertia"), **σ** scales random perturbations (variability), and **dW** is a Wiener process. Key papers: Kuppens, Oravecz & Tuerlinckx, "Feelings change," *JPSP* 99(6):1042–1060 (2010); Oravecz, Tuerlinckx & Vandekerckhove, *Psychological Methods* 16(4):468–490 (2011). Emotional inertia has been linked prospectively to depression: Kuppens, Sheeber, Yap, Whittle, Simmons & Allen (*Emotion* 12(2):283–289, 2012) found in early adolescents (N=165) that "greater inertia of both negative and positive emotional behaviors predicted the emergence of clinical depression 2.5 years later." **Status: accepted/mainstream in quantitative psychology;** active debate over whether nonlinear models (cusp/catastrophe, the Affective Ising Model) outperform the linear OU.

**3. Catastrophe theory — René Thom, E.C. Zeeman.**
Zeeman's **cusp catastrophe** model of aggression (1976) models a dog's behavior (attack/flight) as a behavior variable X on a folded surface controlled by two parameters: **fear** and **rage**. The cusp surface is generated by setting the derivative of a potential function (of the form V = x⁴/4 + ax²/2 + bx) to zero; it captures bimodality, sudden jumps ("catastrophes"), hysteresis, and inaccessibility of intermediate states. Applied to anorexia, attitude change, and stock markets. Scherer uses hysteresis from this tradition in the frustration–aggression relationship. **Status: historically important but largely abandoned in the 1980s** after critiques (Zahler & Sussmann) that it makes few testable predictions and forces discrete variables to be continuous; partially rehabilitated by Cobb's maximum-likelihood statistical fitting and "catastrophe flags."

**4. Component Process Model — Klaus Scherer.**
Scherer defines emotion as "an episode of interrelated, synchronized changes in the states of all or most of five organismic subsystems" (cognitive/appraisal, neurophysiological, motivational, motor-expression, subjective feeling) in response to **sequential appraisal checks** (relevance, implication, coping potential, normative significance). He explicitly advocates a **nonlinear dynamical-systems** treatment ("Emotions are emergent processes," *Phil. Trans. R. Soc. B* 364:3459–3474, 2009), invoking hysteresis and attractor dynamics; emotion is "subsystem synchronization driven by nonlinear appraisal processes." **Status: leading appraisal theory; the dynamical-systems formalization is programmatic rather than a closed model,** though recent work estimates emotion intensity from physiological signals using CPM.

**5. Dynamical systems / self-organization — Marc Lewis.**
Lewis ("Bridging emotion theory and neurobiology through dynamic systems modeling," *Behavioral and Brain Sciences* 28(2):169–194, 2005; and the 2000 Lewis & Granic volume) models emotion as self-organizing, with **trigger, self-amplification, and self-stabilization** phases of "emotional interpretations" that consolidate into traits via nested feedback, neuromodulation, and synaptic plasticity. **Status: influential conceptual framework within developmental/affective science; qualitative more than quantitative.**

**6. Control-theory / cybernetic model of affect — Charles Carver & Michael Scheier.**
Carver & Scheier (*Psychological Bulletin* 92(1):111–135, 1982; and 1990, 1998) propose two nested negative-feedback loops: a first loop reduces the discrepancy between current state and goal; a **second loop monitors the velocity (rate) of discrepancy reduction**, and its output is affect — **positive affect when progress exceeds the reference rate, negative affect when it falls short**. This makes affect literally the error signal on the derivative of a control system. **Status: well-established self-regulation framework with empirical support** (e.g., velocity mediating personality–affect links). This model is also a natural connector to the user's Direction 1, since it grounds affect directly in goal/need pursuit.

**7. Appraisal-based computational models — OCC; Gratch & Marsella's EMA; affective computing.**
Rosalind Picard's *Affective Computing* (MIT Press, 1997) founded the field. The **OCC model** (Ortony, Clore & Collins, 1988) formalizes 22 emotion types via appraisal of events, agents, and objects, and has been implemented in BDI logic and fuzzy-logic systems. **EMA** ("EMotion and Adaptation"; Gratch & Marsella, *Cognitive Systems Research* 5(4):269–306, 2004; Marsella & Gratch, *Cognitive Systems Research* 10(1):70–90, 2009) is a process model of appraisal dynamics computing relevance, desirability, likelihood, blame/credit, and controllability over a "causal interpretation," with emotion intensity ≈ **utility × probability** (desirability × likelihood) and a coping feedback loop. OCC-to-PAD mappings (e.g., ALMA, Gebhard 2005) bridge discrete and dimensional models. **Status: dominant computational-appraisal tradition in AI; engineering formulations rather than empirically unique psychological laws.**

**8. Theory of constructed emotion — Lisa Feldman Barrett.**
Barrett ("The theory of constructed emotion," *Social Cognitive and Affective Neuroscience* 12(1):1–23, 2017) recasts emotions as predictive constructions: the brain, as a hierarchical prediction/active-inference engine, generates emotion concepts to categorize interoceptive and exteroceptive sensory inputs in the service of **allostasis** (energy regulation). It integrates the Embodied Predictive Interoception Coding (EPIC) model with predictive coding. **Status: prominent, influential, contested** (debated against basic-emotion theory); the formal content is borrowed from active inference rather than emotion-specific equations.

**For DIRECTION 2 of the user's project, focus in this staged order:**

1. **Anchor on the Free Energy Principle / active inference as the unifying spine.** It is the only framework with explicit mathematics (variational free energy F = complexity − accuracy) that handles consciousness (Seth, Wiese), emotion (Barrett, Seth's interoceptive inference), AND motivation/needs (allostasis) — directly connecting the user's Direction 1 (needs/motivation) and Direction 2 (consciousness/emotion). Start with Friston (2010, *Nature Reviews Neuroscience*), Seth (2013, *TiCS*), and Barrett (2017, *SCAN*). **Benchmark to advance:** if you can write a single variational objective whose minimization yields both a motivational (need-based) term and an affective (interoceptive-error) term, that is your project's core contribution.

2. **For an explicit, usable "equation for emotion," adopt the Ornstein–Uhlenbeck / DynAffect model.** It is the most empirically validated quantitative emotion model and is tractable with experience-sampling (intensive longitudinal) data. Use Kuppens, Oravecz & Tuerlinckx (2010, 2011). **Benchmark:** test whether a nonlinear (cusp/Affective Ising) extension beats the linear OU on your data — if yes, that connects emotion dynamics back to catastrophe theory (Zeeman) and is publishable.

3. **For consciousness, treat IIT as the reference standard but do not bet on it exclusively.** Read IIT 4.0 (Albantakis et al. 2023) and the 2025 Cogitate *Nature* paper together to understand both the formalism and its empirical limits. **Threshold that should change your plan:** if the ongoing iEEG/monkey adversarial replication further undercuts IIT's posterior-synchronization prediction, pivot toward GNW/active-inference hybrids.

4. **Mine the "structural turn" for overlooked breakthroughs.** The category-theoretic qualia work (Tsuchiya, Saigo, Kleiner; Yoneda lemma) and Hoffman's conscious-agent formalism are where genuinely novel mathematics is being applied and where citations are still sparse. Kleiner's *Entropy* 2020 paper and 2024 thesis are the best entry points and explicitly catalog open problems. **This is the highest-risk, highest-reward area.**

5. **Catalog the fringe deliberately but label it clearly.** McFadden's CEMI, Fisher's Posner-molecule proposal, Stapp's QZE, and Orch-OR should be included for completeness and because the user explicitly wants obscure work — but flag their contested/untested status. Fisher's nuclear-spin proposal is the most scientifically testable of the quantum group and worth watching.

## Caveats

- **No theory of consciousness is established.** Every mathematical theory surveyed is contested, untested, or unfalsifiable in practice. The 2025 adversarial collaboration challenged the two leading theories. Claims that any framework "solves" consciousness should be read as research programs, not results.
- **"Equation for consciousness" vs. "equation for emotion" are very different in maturity.** Emotion models (circumplex, OU/DynAffect, control theory) are empirically grounded and modest; consciousness equations (Φ, conscious agents, perceptronium) are ambitious but practically incomputable or speculative.
- **Beware future-tense and marketing language in sources.** Many popular write-ups (e.g., of Tegmark, Hoffman, Orch-OR) present speculative proposals as if achieved. Hoffman's spacetime-derivation is explicitly incomplete; Tegmark's perceptronium is a thought experiment; Orch-OR's decoherence problem is unresolved (Tegmark vs. Hagan–Hameroff–Tuszyński).
- **Computational ≠ phenomenal.** Many "computational models of emotion" (EMA, OCC) and some consciousness theories (AST) model the *functional or reportable* aspects, not subjective experience itself — important for a project specifically about ý thức (awareness) and cảm xúc (emotion).
- **Source quality varies.** Several specifics here (e.g., IIT toy-model Φ values, conscious-agent polytope dimensions) come from secondary explainers and wikis alongside primary arXiv/journal papers; the primary papers (cited by DOI/arXiv above) should be consulted directly before relying on any specific number.
